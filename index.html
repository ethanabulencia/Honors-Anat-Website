1. Interactive Digestive System Map

The alimentary canal is responsible for breaking down food, absorbing nutrients, and removing waste.

Mouth

Function: Begins mechanical and chemical digestion.

Enzymes: Salivary amylase breaks down carbohydrates.

Pharynx

Function: Passageway for food moving from mouth to esophagus.

Process: Swallowing.

Esophagus

Function: Moves food to the stomach.

Process: Peristalsis.

Stomach

Function: Mixes food with gastric juices.

Enzymes: Pepsin digests proteins.

Small Intestine

Function: Main site of nutrient absorption.

Enzymes: Pancreatic enzymes and bile help digestion.

Large Intestine

Function: Absorbs water and forms feces.

Process: Bacterial fermentation.

Rectum/Anus

Function: Stores and eliminates waste.

Process: Excretion.

2. Phases of Digestion

Ingestion

  • Food enters the mouth.
  • Chewing mechanically breaks food apart.
  • Saliva begins carbohydrate digestion.

Digestion

  • Occurs in the stomach and small intestine.
  • Proteins, fats, and carbohydrates are chemically broken down.
  • Enzymes and acids assist digestion.

Absorption

  • Mainly occurs in the small intestine.
  • Nutrients enter the bloodstream through villi.
  • Water absorption continues in the large intestine.

Excretion

  • Undigested waste leaves the body.
  • Occurs through the rectum and anus.

BMR Calculation

Harris-Benedict Equation:

BMR = 66 + (6.23 × weight in lbs) + (12.7 × height in inches) − (6.8 × age)

BMR = 66 + (6.23 × 160) + (12.7 × 70) − (6.8 × 17)

BMR = 1837 calories/day

TMR Calculation

TMR = BMR × Activity Multiplier

TMR = 1837 × 1.55

TMR = 2847 calories/day

4. Diet Research & Nutritional Goals

Mediterranean Diet

  • Focuses on fruits, vegetables, fish, olive oil, and whole grains.
  • Supports heart health and healthy digestion.
  • Rich in healthy fats and fiber.
  • Can be expensive and requires meal preparation.

Why It Fits the Subject

The Mediterranean diet supports muscle growth and overall health by providing lean proteins, healthy fats, and nutrient-dense foods.

Nutritional Goals

  • Calories: 2800/day
  • Protein: 150g/day
